Gidon, 14, caps hospital parking fees

Chronically ill teen Gidon has capped hospital parking fees, impacting thousands of NSW patients, following a campaign where 70,000 people signed his petition. Thanks to his win, many repeat patients will pay ten times less in hospital parking fees, saving $200 a week. NSW Premier Gladys Berejiklian said: "Sometimes it takes people like Gidon to start a movement on an important issue to nudge government into change." It was also front page news, with the Daily Telegraph editorial calling on the government to hire 14-year-old Gidon as "a consultant with a proven track record for getting things done."  More

'Gay panic' abolished as legal defence for murder in QLD

Catholic Priest Father Paul Kelly started a petition to abolish 'gay panic' as a legal defence for murder after Wayne Ruks was killed in his churchyard. His killers used this defence in court, claiming that a 'homosexual advance' was made at them. Their murder sentences were downgraded to manslaughter. One walked free after four years. Father Kelly's victory would take five years and 290,000 signatures, making it one of the biggest petition victories in Australian history.  More

Gracelyn’s petition convinced QLD government to drop an appeal that defended ‘racist’ cops

Gracelyn wrote this victory note to her signers: "A few months ago the QLD government was set on appealing the Federal court decision that found police to have acted with racism in 2004. An Aboriginal man had just died in custody, and the community protested before the police responded with awful violence. The Attorney General appealed this decision, and that the government was gearing up to defend the ‘racism’ of QLD police. So I started my Change.org petition, you all added your signatures and took online actions to convince the government to drop the appeal. And today, we won! This is huge justice for my Aboriginal community, and that of Palm Island."  More
